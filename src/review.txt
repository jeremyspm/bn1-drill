CELLDIV-5 | Read and answer the material on cell division from the following pages. Cell division in the body: Mitosis and Meiosis There are two types of cell division called mitosis and meiosis.Mitosis is essentially for the growth and repair of all living tissues in humans, whereas meiosis only occurs in the special reproductive glands – specifically in the testes and ovaries in humans. (These processes do occur in animals and also plants as well). New cells form from old ones by cell division, allowing organisms to grow and repair by replacing worn out and damaged tissues and cells. When cells become too big, it is difficult for oxygen and nutrients to diffuse across the large cell. As the cell becomes inefficient, it will divide into two smaller cells. Thus, mitotic cell division is necessary when cells become too large. Once mitotic cell division is completed, two new cells would have formed: each with exactly the same number of chromosomes as the mother cell. During meiosis, four new cells are made. Each of these cells has half the number of chromosomes as the original cell. That is why meiosis is also known as “reduction” devision. Meiosis only occurs in organisms that reproduce sexually. For example, in humans, during fertilisation the sperm cell from the male with 23 chromosomes unites with the egg from the female which also has 23 chromosomes to form one cell with 46 chromosomes. Because sexual reproduction involves the joining together of these two gametes, the number of chromosomes in each gamete must be halved so that when the gametes join, the correct number of chromosomes will be maintained.
